Nutrir — Meal Verification CV

Production · Adopted

Computer-vision pipeline for meal verification deployed by the Municipality of Fortaleza — classifies meal components from a photo and pairs the percentages with a scale weight to produce per-meal nutritional records.

Problem

The Municipality of Fortaleza distributes meals ("quentinhas") to vulnerable populations and needs an objective record that each meal contains the required food groups in the right proportions. Manual verification across thousands of meals doesn't scale, and there is no way to audit historical batches without photos and measurements.

Architecture

Mobile photo + scale weight per meal feeds an image-segmentation model that classifies regions into food categories (rice, beans, meat, vegetables, other) and estimates each category's volume percentage. Output is structured JSON: per-food description plus `percentual_volume` plus `peso_total_refeicao` in grams — auditable, queryable, and ready for downstream dashboards.

Tech Stack

  • Python
  • PyTorch
  • image segmentation
  • OpenCV

Python, PyTorch, image segmentation models, OpenCV. Output is a structured JSON record per meal photo, with the meal's total weight read from a calibration scale.

Engineering Highlights

  • Pairs CV output (food-category percentages) with a calibration scale weight to produce gram-level totals, not just relative proportions.
  • Generates structured, auditable JSON per meal — the pipeline is the source of truth for nutritional auditing, not a one-off model.
  • Image-segmentation model trained on labeled regional Brazilian meal compositions.

Outcomes

  • Deployed and adopted by the Municipality of Fortaleza for meal-distribution programs.
  • Replaces hand-counting with a queryable, auditable record of every meal's composition and weight.
